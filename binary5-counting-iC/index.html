Binary Lesson 5 Counting

Power of 2

  • One bit has 2 possible values (2 1)
    • 0 or 1
  • Two bits have 4 possible values (2 2)
    • 00 01 11 10
  • Three bits have 8 values (2 3)
    • 000 001 101 011 100 101 110 111
  • Four bits have 16 values (2 4)
    • 0000 0001 ... 1110 1111

A Byte

  • 8 bits, from 0000000 to 11111111
  • 2 8 combinations
  • 2x2x2x2x2x2x2x2 = 256
  • In Hex, 00 to FF
  • In Hex, 00 to FF
  • 16 x 16 = 256
  • 2 8 = 2 4 x 2 4

A word - 16 Bits

  • 0000 to FFFF
  • 2 16 values = 65,536
  • 2 16 = 2 8 x 2 8 = 256 x 256
  • 2 16 = 2 4 x 2 4 x 2 4 x 2 4
  •          = 16 x 16 x 16 x 16

The Easy Way

  • 2 10 - 1,024, written as 1K
  • So 16 bits have 2 16 values
  • 2 16 = 2 6 x 2 10 = 2 6000 = 64 K
  • Approximately 64,000

32 Bits

  • Range of values from
  • 0000:0000 to FFFF:FFFF
  • 2 32 values
  • 2 2 x 2 10 x 2 10 x 2 10
  • 4 K K K = 4 K M = 4 G
  • Approximately 4,000,000,000

64 Bits

  • 0000:0000:0000:0000 to 1111:1111:1111:1111
  • 2 64
  • 2 4 x 2 10 x 2 10 x 2 10 x 2 10 x 2 10 x 2 10
  • 16 K K K K K K = 16 G G
  • Approximately
  • 16,000,000,000,000,000,000

128 Bits

  • The entire IPv6 address space, from 0000:0000:0000:0000:0000:0000:0000:0000
    to
    ffff:ffff:ffff:ffff:ffff:ffff:ffff:ffff
  • 2 128 = 2 8 x 2 128
  • 2 8 + 2 30 + 2 30 + 2 30 + 2 30
  • 256 G G G G
  • Approximately 256,000,000,000,000,000,000,000,000,000,000,000,000

Slash Notation

  • fe80::/64 means 64 bits are fixed, so 64 bits vary
    • 2 64 addresses
  • fe80::/16 means 16 bits are fixed, so 112 bits vary
    • 2 112 addresses
  • 2620:1:b::/48 means 48 bits are fixed, so 80 bits vary
    • 2 80 addresses
